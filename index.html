About Me

I am a dedicated PhD Researcher in Mechanics and Materials, based at **Clermont Auvergne**. My core specialization lies in the study of the **thermo-mechanical behavior of Shape Memory Alloys (SMAs)** using advanced experimental techniques.

My work combines solid theoretical understanding in continuum mechanics and applied thermodynamics with practical expertise in full-field measurement methods and numerical simulation (Finite Element Methods - FEM).

I am highly proficient in programming for scientific computing, particularly in **Python**, **C**, and **C++**, and utilize specialized software like **ANSYS** for complex simulations. I thrive on rigorous analysis, independent problem-solving, and collaborative team environments.

Research & Technical Projects

Showcasing my work in experimental mechanics, numerical methods, and scientific programming.

PhD Research: Shape Memory Alloys

Clermont Auvergne | Oct 2025 - Present

Experimental study of the thermo-mechanical behavior of Shape Memory Alloys (SMAs) using coupled, full-field measurement techniques.

  • Development of advanced full-field measurement methods (e.g., Digital Image Correlation, Localized Spectral Analysis - LSA).
  • Coupling of experimental techniques (e.g., LSA - Infrared Thermography) for comprehensive thermo-mechanical analysis.
  • In-depth analysis and exploitation of complex experimental data.

Master 2 Thesis: LSA & Thermography

Institut Pascal (Master 2 MMF) | Apr 2025 - Sep 2025

Implementation and application of Localized Spectral Analysis (LSA) for precise displacement field measurement in experimental mechanics.

  • Mastered and implemented the LSA method for field measurement.
  • Developed the experimental coupling system between LSA and infrared thermography.
  • Utilized OpenLSA code for image processing and post-treatment of experimental data.

Technical Project: Contact Mechanics Simulation

Study of surface coating effects, focusing on **fretting** and **Hertz contact** simulations. This involved creating complex geometry and applying boundary conditions within a commercial Finite Element software.

  • Software: ANSYS
  • Methods: Fretting analysis, Hertzian contact modeling.

Technical Project: Chemical Compound Convection Simulation

Numerical simulation of chemical compound convention using programming methods. Demonstrating capability in translating physical phenomena into computational models.

  • Methods: Finite Volume Method.
  • Programming: Python implementation.
